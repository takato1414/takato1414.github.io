In the domains of mobility and life-support robotics, this joint research focuses on the foundations of learning and inference required for humans and robots to coordinate while adapting to each other.

We work on imitation and reinforcement learning, robot behavior acquisition using foundation models such as Large Language Models and Vision-Language Models, and models of mutual adaptation between humans and robots based on active inference. Through task designs targeting real robots and real-world environments, we contribute to the realization of intelligent robots that can be widely useful in society.
